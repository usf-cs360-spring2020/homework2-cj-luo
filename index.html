Purposes

Visualize the “Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates of Child and Parent Income Distributions by College” dataset from the “Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility” project from Opportunity Insights.

Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility

We document four results. First, access to colleges varies greatly by parent income. For example, children whose parents are in the top 1% of the income distribution are 77 times more likely to attend an Ivy League college than those whose parents are in the bottom income quintile

Visualizations

Sum of Par Q5, sum of Par Q4, sum of Par Q3, sum of Par Q2 and sum of Par Q1 vs. sum of K Q5, sum of K Q4, sum of K Q3, sum of K Q2 and sum of K Q1. Color shows details about Tier. Details are shown for Name. The data is filtered on State, which keeps CA.

The general idea is the parents income have a colse relationship with thier kids income. As we can see from the scatter plot, The higher income of parents which also means thier children have more opportunity to enroll in lvy college(tier type is 1), higher change have more income in their funture.
